After Thoughts - March 1997

Let's Make A List

Here's a great new game you can play at your next party. It's called, "Let's Make a List". Just make a list of all the things evolutionists used to believe, but don't believe any longer. The person with the longest list wins. A typical list might begin like this:
  1. The Earth is 100 million years old.
  2. The Earth is 500 million years old.
  3. The Earth is 2 billion years old.
  4. Neanderthal Man is the missing link.
  5. Nebraska Man is the missing link.
  6. Java Man is the missing link.
  7. Piltdown Man is the missing link.
  8. Cro-Magnon Man is the missing link.
  9. The evolution of the horse.
  10. Dinosaurs were cold-blooded.
  11. Dinosaurs were slow and clumsy.
  12. The Coelacanth fish is the ancestor of an amphibian.
  13. Archaeopteryx is the link between reptiles and birds.
  14. Acquired traits can be inherited.
  15. etc.

If you want to play a more challenging version of the game, try making a list of all the things evolutionists believe which can be shown to be true. For example,

  1. Natural selection causes small variations in species.
